Which regions in Europe provide children the greatest opportunity to exceed the educational outcomes of their parents? To what extent is this driven by changes in the educational distribution? 

This website presents "The European Atlas of Spatially Disaggregated Intergenerational Mobility" (EUROPE-IGM-ATLAS
). The EUROPE-IGM-ATLAS provides researchers, policymakers, and regional leaders with the first spatially disaggregated longitudinal evidence on intergenerational educational mobility across European regions over time.

The database is available in two forms: cohort level measures (for children born 1940-59, 1960-79, and 1980-99), and contemporary measures of effective mobility based on weighted composite indices (1985-2025).
